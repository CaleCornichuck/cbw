Cryptography

Cryptography is the study or act of securing data and communications. There are many ways to secure the data being sent, such as encryption, or algorithms.


Crpytography comes from the Greek word kryptos, which means hidden, or secret.


Encryption

Encryption is a very common way to secure data. When converting the data, cryptographers use a secret code to turn the data into something more secure. This makes it harder to decipher if the data falls into the wrong hands.


Ciphers

Cryptographers most commonly use cryptographic algorithms to secure any messages that are being sent across communications, known as ciphers. There are many different kinds of ciphers that are used, each with their own purpose. These ciphers include:

  • Hash Functions

  • Symmetric-key Algorithms

  • Asymmetric-key Algorithms
