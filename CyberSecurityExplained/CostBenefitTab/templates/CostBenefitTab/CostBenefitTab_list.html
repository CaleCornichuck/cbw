Costs of Attacks

Companies lost $1 trillion dollars in 2020 from cyber attacks. These numbers have been increasing since 2018. Let’s break down the numbers to what a single company loses in one year. These are average numbers, and each company may experience something different. A larger company tends to lose more money than a smaller company. For example, hackers can ask for more money from larger companies. Here is what companies lost in 2020.

  • Companies with 1-49 employees lost an average of $24,000.
  • Companies with 50-249 employees lost an average of $50,000.
  • Companies with 250-999 employees lost an average of $133,000.
  • Companies with 1000 or more employees lost an average of $504,000.

Having good Cyber Security can save companies a lot of money and is a must. With growing reliance on technology, we can be sure the threat of attacks is at its highest. Companies need to ask themselves if they want to pay now or pay later.



Costs of Security

The global spending on cyber security in 2020 was $123 billion and is expected to go up. Of course, each business spends a different amount, and each company needs to do a risk assessment. A study done by Deloitte and the Financial Services Information Sharing and Analysis Center concluded that financial services use 10% of the IT budget on cyber security. This 10% is only 0.2% to 0.9% of the company’s revenue. The President’s budget in 2019 spent 15 billion on cyber security only 0.3% of the budget. Now, a more average cost of cyber security for companies can be very different from a small business to a large business. On average a company will spend 6% – 14% of the IT budget on cyber security. The IT budget is 3.2% on average for a company of all sizes. So, if all companies spend 10% of their IT budgets on cyber security, the estimated amount spent is the following.

  • Large companies spend $2 million to $5 million per year.
  • Medium size companies spend $500,000 to $2 million per year.
  • Small companies spend $500,000 and less per year.

Phishing is the most common cyber security attack.



Risk Assessment

The best place to start is with a risk assessment so the company has an idea of what kind of cyber security they should invest in.

  • What are the companies’ priorities?
  • How is data stored or collected?
  • Does the company have vulnerabilities?
  • Are there specific attacks the company is at risk for such as, human error or phishing?
  • How damaging would it be to have a data leakage?
