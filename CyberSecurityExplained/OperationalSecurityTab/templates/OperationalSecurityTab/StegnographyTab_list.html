Steganography



Steganography is the practice of concealing data, or some form of message, by embedding it behind other text or images. While this may sound similar to cryptography it remains an entirely different science and is particularly devious as it requires no encryption or encoding. In general, the medium in which our message is encoded consists of bits, some of which are redundant to the composition of it. These redundant bits can be repurposed to hold new data, thereby hiding our actual message. The trick to this is doing so without deforming the medium and revealing it was tampered with.


Additionally, every photo contains meta-data which are various details encoded into it automatically. Typically, the time and location of when the photo was taken. This online tool can show the meta-data of images.
