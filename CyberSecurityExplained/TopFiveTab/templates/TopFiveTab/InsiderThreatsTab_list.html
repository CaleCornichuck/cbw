What and Who are Insider Threats?

Insider threats are individuals that have access to a computer system or network within a company and expose the company to malicious code. These individuals can knowingly exfiltrate data for personal gain or can be negligent and expose the company to a hacker. These individuals do not have to be a current employee all they need is inside access to the company’s system or network. Not all individuals are careless or clueless some actually get tricked. Social engineering attacks are when employees are manipulated and end up disclosing confidential information. Which is then used maliciously to gain access to a companies system or network.

Let’s look at some examples below of how Malicious and Negligent individuals can be insider threats.

Malicious Insiders

Malicious insiders are responsible for 14% of all incidents.

  • The disgruntled insider decides to delete important data to get revenge for maybe losing their job or not getting something, they wanted.
  • They sell data and information to other parties for money.
  • The insider gives data and information to another company for personal gain or money to help the company achieve more success.
  • The insider exposes the company systems to malware that can spread and cause damage out of revenge.
  • They can also physically steal hard drives to sell, leak, or use for personal gain.
Negligent Insiders

Negligent Insiders are responsible for 87% of all incidents. 25% of these incidents are related to physically stolen credentials or credentials accessed by phishing. 62% of these incidents are when the negligent insider sends emails to the wrong person.

  • This negligent insider can fail to secure a database properly. Then exposing personal and private information of customers for anyone to see.
  • A current employee can fall for phishing attacks by clicking on emails with malware. They can also receive a phone call from a hacker asking for credentials or information to gain inside access to the company’s system or network.
  • One simple and common mistake the insider can make is sending the wrong person an email with private and personal information which can be used or leaked for personal gain.
  • Another mistake is leaving a google drive open with private and personal information for anyone with a link to access.
